Multimedia

Arete Living Arts presents multimedia performances on an ongoing basis. The performances mix video art projections with music, dance, poetry, theater, and audience participation. The productions are group projects that combine the talents of many artists and technicians in many different mediums.

The video and film works featured in the productions add a layer of meaning to the live stage action, and often act as the main focus of the presentations with video works that not only interact, but lead the live performers.

The video and film works are created by a wide variety of filmmakers. Most works are created specifically for the productions.

Music

Arete Living Arts has presented dozens of music concerts. Music performance is the foundation of Arete's work. Performances have included everything from rock to jazz, classical, folk, and experimental forms. Venues have ranged from small art galleries to large concert halls. Arete's mission is to support and promote emerging musicians who are dedicated to technical and creative excellence.

Music is the base that Arete's multimedia productions are built on. Often productions begin with music and the other mediums are used as a means to illustrate the message within the music. Musically, Arete artists mix and match musical styles to create new sounds and ideas. As always, Arete strives to support artists who are true to their artistic vision and have a message of value that they express in their music.



Theatre

Theater, performance art, comedy, and drama are vital elements of Arete Living Arts productions. Arete productions usually mix elements to create experimental forms. Often performances attempt to keep the audience guessing whether stage action is scripted for the actors or a spontaneous expression of true emotions. Actors are expected to control these fine lines in order to achieve the artistic goal.

Arete productions also regularly break the fourth wall to make the audience an integral part of the performance. When audience reaction is central to the movement of the production, the actors are required to improvise while maintaining the overall direction of the work. The skills required of actors are far beyond anything most actors encounter.


Dance


Dance is always a part of Arete's multimedia performances. Arete has worked with talented choreographers such as Heather Bell and Amy Patierno, and a long list of dancers including Samantha D, Lilah, Shamira, Jody Lelac, Ivette Oliveras, Chisa, and The Dazzle Dancers.



Fashion



Arete has worked with many talented designers. Vilma Mare's day-glo-a-go-go suits were designed specifically for Arete dancers, and Paul George's campy beach-suits were created for a performance in New York City. Other costumes have been designed by Mhina dada, Samantha D, and C.V.C. Fashions.


Arete has also presented some of the fashion world's most cutting edge designers at their fashion shows. A recent show at New York's Webster Hall featured Garo Sparo and Gaelyn and Cianfarani. All Arete performances feature costumes by emerging designers. In addition to Webster Hall, fashion shows have been held at venues such Downtime and Maxwells.



Poetry


Poetry and spoken word has been a part of Arete Living Art's multimedia shows since inception. At times poetry is presented with interactive video playing the role of poet. Arete Living Arts has also been presenting poetry events since 1996 when Caeser Pink was host of Maxwell's Poetry Society performance night.


Since that time Arete's poetry event have been held at venues such as Downtime, The Stephen Gang Gallery, and The Heather Milburn Salon Space. Among the talented poets that Arete has presented are K-Rob, Pink Diamond, Nile Goddess, Pink Diamond, Brother Earl, Vanessa Hidary, Nacinimod, Sue Moss, and Mo Beasley. Arete also releases CDs of poetry and spoken word recordings.




Street Theatre


Street theatre has a long history of use for activism. Street Theatre often uses the element of surprise to engage the public. Arete Living Arts employed street theatre techniques for their protest performance art piece titled Our Daily Bread.


What is "Our Daily Bread"?


The Our Daily Bread performance was presented in the area of the New York Stock Exchange. "Our Daily Bread" is a comedic social experiment exploring the issues of materialism and liberty in the modern corporate world. In the piece, elaborately masked and costumed actors ceremoniously placed loaves of fresh white bread along the sidewalks of the financial district. Each loaf of bread had a letter from Satan attached that offered to buy the reader's souls in exchange for the material possessions won through climbing the corporate ladder. When faced with the spectacle, the busy executives looked bewildered. Soon police were called in and bomb sniffing police dogs were put to the task of inspecting the harmless loaves of bread. When passers by asked what the event was all about, the performers dryly replied that they were 'distributing food to the rich.' Although reactions to the performance ranged from laughter to anger, by the end of the day tourists were lined up to take pictures with the performers in front of the statue of George Washington (a loaf of bread was, of course, placed strategically at George's feet,) and the gray facade of Wall Street was enlivened by the colorful presence of the performance
